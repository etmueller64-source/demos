Programs · High Performance

HP

A core branch of AKBA's Player Development Pipeline — preparing committed athletes for Provincial, College, University, and other advanced competitive pathways.

Ages

12U–18U

Elite development for athletes who show a strong commitment to high-level competition and long-term athletic growth, structured across two stages of the pipeline.

What to expect

Season format

  • Format: Tryout-based, elite competitive
  • Season: Year-round — mandatory off-season plus spring/summer season
  • Commitment: 4+ sessions per week
The pipeline

Player Development Pipeline

A structured, multi-stage development journey built to advance athletes to the highest levels of amateur baseball.

12U–14U

Development HP

  • Advanced skill development, game strategy, situational play, and strength & conditioning
  • Introduces mental performance and leadership
  • High-level league and tournament play
  • Accommodates two-sport athletes
  • 12U & 13U compete in the interlock AAA loop with YSBA, TBA, and EOBA
  • 14U may compete in the EBLO starting in 2027
15U–18U

HP

  • High-performance training environment with leadership and mentorship
  • Advanced competition — Provincial, National, and Showcase
  • Direct college/university pathway preparation
  • Competes in the EBLO
Mandatory program

Off-season development

Every HP team takes part in a comprehensive off-season training program.

1

Fall Ball

A fall schedule of at least six (6) games, weather permitting.

2

Strength & Conditioning

Structured sessions led by a certified strength and conditioning coach, plus independent routines.

3

Baseball Skills Development

At least five (5) hours of organized training per week — hitting, pitching, catching, fielding, throwing, and baseball-specific athleticism.

4

Mental Performance Training

At least two (2) large-group sessions per off-season covering focus, confidence, resilience, goal-setting, and leadership.

5

Nutrition Education

At least two (2) annual sessions delivered by a qualified professional.

6

Individual Development Plans

Every athlete gets a customized IDP covering strengths, areas for improvement, goals, and progress benchmarks.

Selection

Tryouts & rosters

How HP teams are formed each season.

Tryout guidelines

Open, fair, and transparent

HP tryouts are publicly advertised, open to all eligible athletes, and conducted fairly. Private tryouts can be coordinated directly with each team's coach. Independent evaluators are used whenever reasonably possible to ensure unbiased selection.

Roster sizes

Minimum & maximum per group

  • 12U–14U: Minimum 12, maximum 14 players
  • 15U–18U: Minimum 12, maximum 17 players
Commitments

Expectations for players & families

What it takes to hold a roster spot on an HP team.

Player & family commitment

A significant dedication of time

Accepting an HP roster spot requires regular attendance at all practices, training sessions, games, and tournaments, along with respectful conduct as a representative of AKBA.

Tournament schedule

At least four events a year

  • Minimum of four (4) tournaments annually, not including mandatory championship events like OBA Eliminations or Provincial Championships
  • Schedules may include showcase events in the United States or elsewhere in Canada
